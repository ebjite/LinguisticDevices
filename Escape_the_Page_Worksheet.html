1
Same consonant sound repeated at the start of nearby words. "Peter Piper picked..."
2
Words with matching end sounds, usually at the end of lines. "bright / night"
3
A pattern of stressed and unstressed syllables — the beat under the words. "DA-da-DA-da"
4
A word or phrase repeated deliberately for emphasis or emotional effect. "We don't need... We don't need..."
5
A comparison using LIKE or AS to link two different things. "Life is like a river"
6
Saying something IS something else — no like or as. Creates a vivid picture. "The city is a beast"

[Stanza 1]
Line 1 The sun rose high... →
Try: Metaphor or Simile Could the sun be something else? A beast? An eye? A door opening?
Or — what does it rise like? "The sun clawed its way up like a burning eye..."
Line 2 The rain came down... →
Try: Imagery Make us feel it. Cold? Heavy? Loud on a roof? Smell of wet earth?
Try words like: hammered · whispered · clattered · swallowed · drummed
Line 3 She ate rice. →
Try: Imagery (all 5 senses) What did it smell like? Sound like? Feel like in her mouth?
Try: "She lifted the first grain to her lips, steam rising like a small ghost..."
Or go longer — this was our class example!
Line 4 The city was loud. She was tired. →
Try: Simile — use LIKE or AS Did you use the word LIKE or AS in your sentence? If not, it's not a simile!
"The city roared like a market that never slept. She was hollow as an empty jar."
[Stanza 2]
Line 5 She ran to catch the bus. →
Try: Alliteration Replace as many words as possible with ones starting with the same letter.
What letter will you choose? S? B? C?
"She sprinted, stumbling, struggling for the steel-and-smoke bus..."
Line 6 He walked to the shop. →
Try: Alliteration — this was our class example! "He bounced brazenly around for the big beautiful bargain bin to buy a bounty."
Pick your own letter and go wild. Keep the meaning — just change the words.
Line 7 The dog slept in the sun. →
Try: Metaphor or Personification What if the dog IS something? Or the sun IS something?
"The dog dissolved into the afternoon, a warm comma on the pavement."
Or make the sun do something human — shine? Watch? Press down?
Line 8 He heard music from outside. →
Try: Imagery + Simile What did the music sound like? Feel like on his skin? Smell like?
Try onomatopoeia — words that sound like what they describe: thrum, hum, pulse, drift, bleed
"The bass hummed through the walls like a second heartbeat."
[Ending]
Ending OK. Goodbye. →
Try: Repetition The ending should land. Repeat something from earlier. Or use a short, punchy rhythm.
"And still the sun rose. And still the rain fell. And still — silence."
Or go simple and powerful: three words. Make them count.
