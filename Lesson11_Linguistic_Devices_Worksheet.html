Alliteration

Every word pulls its weight

Example
"He walked to the shop"
= He bounced brazenly around for the big beautiful bargain bin to buy a bounty.
"She ran to catch the bus"
Rewrite so as many words as possible share the same starting sound
"The dog slept in the sun"
Any letter — keep the meaning, change the words
Imagery

Make them feel it, smell it, taste it

Example
"He drank hot tea"
= He raised the golden vessel to his lips, the steam wafting to his glasses, making the world cloud up like a dream state, the soft silky liquid cartwheeling down his throat — and with a great big gulp: ah. Satisfaction was his.
"She ate rice."
Expand into 2–4 lines. Use all 5 senses: sight, sound, smell, taste, touch.
"He heard music from outside."
Expand — what does it smell like? Feel like? What colour is it?

Hyperbole
Extreme exaggeration used for effect — not meant literally.

Oxymoron
Two contradictory words placed together to create meaning.

Enjambment
A sentence that continues past the end of a line without a pause — builds momentum.
"I have eaten / the plums / that were in / the icebox"

Caesura
A deliberate pause mid-line, often shown by punctuation.
"To be, ‖ or not to be, that is the question."
