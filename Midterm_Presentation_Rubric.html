Story & Structure
Wallas: all 4 stages
 Clear narrative arc through all 4 Wallas stages. Inspiration, exploration, decision and reflection all present and connected. Message held until the final slide. Most stages present and connected. Reveal is at or near the end. Basic structure present but stages feel disconnected or out of order. Reveal may come too early. Little or no structure. Stages missing. No narrative arc.
Concept & Intent
Hall: encoding
 Concept is clear and personally meaningful. Intended emotions are specific and explained. Message held until the final slide. Concept is clear. Emotions mentioned but not fully explained. Message mostly held. Concept is vague. Emotions not clearly linked to artistic decisions. Message revealed too early. Concept unclear or not stated. No discussion of intent or message.
Technical Knowledge
 Materials, process and tools described accurately and specifically. Shows genuine understanding of craft decisions made. Materials and process described with some specificity. Minor gaps but overall clear. General description only. Missing key technical details or inaccurate about process. Very limited or no technical description. Process unclear.
Reflection
Wallas: verification
 Genuine, specific reflection on what worked and what did not. Intended vs actual outcome compared honestly. Concrete improvement identified. Some reflection present. Comparison of intent and result attempted. Improvement mentioned. Reflection is superficial. No real comparison of intent and result. No meaningful reflection or self-assessment.
Evidence & Research
 Clear evidence of research and investigation. Artist references specific and well chosen. Work is grounded in genuine exploration beyond initial idea. Some research evident. Artist references present. Could be more specific or developed. Minimal research. References vague or generic. Feels underprepared. No evidence of research or investigation.
