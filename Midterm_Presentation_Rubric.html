Story & Structure
Wallas: all 4 stages
 Clear narrative arc through all 4 Wallas stages. Inspiration, exploration, decision and reflection all present and connected. Reveal held until the end. Most stages present. Some connections between stages. Reveal is at or near the end. Basic structure present but stages feel disconnected or out of order. Reveal may come too early. Little or no structure. Stages missing or unclear. No narrative arc.
Concept & Intent
Hall: encoding
 Concept is clear and personally meaningful. Intended emotions are specific and explained. Genre identified. Message held until the final slide. Concept is clear. Emotions and genre mentioned but not fully explained. Message mostly held. Concept is vague or generic. Emotions not clearly linked to artistic decisions. Message revealed too early. Concept unclear or not stated. No discussion of intent or message.
Technical Knowledge
 Materials, process and tools described accurately and specifically. Shows genuine understanding of the medium and craft decisions made. Materials and process described. Some specificity. One or two gaps but overall clear. General description only. Missing key technical details or inaccurate about process. Very limited or no technical description. Process unclear.
Reflection & Honesty
Wallas: verification
 Genuine, specific reflection on what worked and what did not. Intended vs actual outcome compared honestly. Concrete improvement identified. Some reflection present. Comparison of intent and result attempted. Improvement mentioned. Reflection is superficial ("it was good"). No comparison of intent and result. Improvement not addressed. No meaningful reflection. No self-assessment.
Audience & Theory
Hall: decoding
 Audience response discussed with reference to Hall's theory. Demonstrates understanding that different viewers decode differently. Cultural or personal context considered. Audience response mentioned. Some connection to Hall's theory attempted. Decoding referenced. Audience mentioned but no theoretical connection made. Hall's theory not used or used incorrectly. No discussion of audience or decoding theory.
AI Documentation
 All AI use clearly declared with specific prompts, outputs and reflections on how AI shaped decisions. Distinction between AI as tool vs substitute is explicit. AI use declared. Some prompts or outputs described. Could be more specific. AI use declared but vaguely. No reflection on how it influenced work. AI used but not declared, OR AI used as a substitute for making the artwork.
Delivery
 Confident, clear and engaging. Speaks to the audience, not the screen. Timing within 4–6 minutes. Invites questions. Mostly confident. Occasional reading from notes. Timing slightly off but close. Questions welcomed. Hesitant or reads entirely from notes. Hard to follow at times. Timing significantly over or under. Very difficult to follow. Inaudible or very short. No audience engagement.
